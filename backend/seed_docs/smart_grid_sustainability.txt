SMART CITY UTILITIES & SUSTAINABLE ENERGY DEPT.
GUIDELINES FOR RESIDENTIAL SMART GRID SUSTAINABILITY
Document Reference: SC-SG-2025-V4
Section 1: The Smart Meter Program
All residential housing units in Smart City are equipped with Smart Grid meters that transmit real-time electricity consumption data to the central city database. This allows for automated billing, peak demand profiling, and consumer visibility.

Section 2: Electricity Consumption Tier Rates
Smart City Utilities uses a tiered pricing system to encourage conservation of electrical energy:
- Tier 1 (Baseline Conservation): Up to 350 kWh per month is billed at the baseline rate of $0.11 per kWh.
- Tier 2 (Standard Usage): 351 to 750 kWh per month is billed at the rate of $0.16 per kWh.
- Tier 3 (High Demand): Usage exceeding 750 kWh per month is billed at the high-usage rate of $0.24 per kWh.

Section 3: Peak Demand Hours and Surcharges
To manage grid stability during high load periods, a peak demand surcharge is applied on weekdays:
- Peak Hours: 2:00 PM to 7:00 PM (Monday through Friday).
- During peak hours, an additional surcharge of $0.05 per kWh is applied to Tier 2 and Tier 3 usage.
- Off-Peak Hours: All other hours, including weekends and federal holidays, are billed at standard tier rates without surcharges.

Section 4: Sustainability Energy Bonus Incentives
Residents who install solar panels connected to the municipal grid can participate in Net Metering. Excess energy fed back into the grid is credited to the resident's account at $0.08 per kWh. 
Additionally, residents who keep their monthly electricity consumption under 300 kWh for six consecutive months are awarded a one-time "Energy Conservation Credit" of $50 on their utility bill.
